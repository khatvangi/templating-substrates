# Sciscape prompt: D6 sharpened — real Mode 6 inheritance mechanism

This prompt supersedes the original Block D6 entry from
`sciscape_lit_search_prompts.md`. It's sharpened with the H3
inheritance-mechanism vocabulary so the search results directly
inform whether real Mode 6 systems implement M0 (true stateless),
M1 (lineage-level fixed templates), or M2 (lineage-level fixation
with re-draw).

---

## D6-revised. Real Mode 6 inheritance mechanism

> The framework I am developing identifies five attested templating
> modes; Mode 6 (2D surface-position templating) is attested in
> materials science but absent from biology. Recent simulation work
> (Test H3 in our project) refined the framework's inheritance theorem
> and identified five distinct inheritance mechanism classes: M0 (true
> stateless: phenotype freshly drawn each generation, no lineage
> state); M1 (lineage-level fixed templates: each lineage carries a
> fixed surface, no mutation, no re-draw); M2 (lineage-level fixation
> with periodic re-draw at branching, rate r ∈ [0,1]); M3 (individual-
> level exact copying without mutation); M4 (individual-level copying
> with mutation, the canonical sequence-template mode). The framework's
> claim is that real Mode 6 systems implement M0-class inheritance, but
> this is not empirically established.
>
> Search the literature on biological surface templating systems and
> related substrates for evidence of which inheritance mechanism class
> each implements. Specifically:
>
> 1. **S-layers (bacterial and archaeal):** Do daughter cells inherit
>    the parent's S-layer pattern (M1-class), or does the pattern
>    re-form from scratch each generation (M0-class)? Include Sleytr,
>    Sára, Pum lab work; van Wely on archaeal S-layers; any
>    self-assembly experiments showing whether nucleation is
>    template-directed.
>
> 2. **Virus capsid templating:** Do progeny capsids inherit
>    geometric or compositional information from parent capsids beyond
>    the genome, or is each progeny capsid built from scratch using
>    only the genome's encoded protein sequence (Mode 1, not Mode 6)?
>    Include any work proposing capsid "memory" or template-directed
>    capsid self-assembly.
>
> 3. **Quasicrystal-templated adlayers and surface-directed crystal
>    growth (Sharma, Smerdon, McGrath, Cuevas):** When a templating
>    surface is duplicated (e.g., via crystal growth or layer
>    transfer), is the templating capacity duplicated, or only the
>    geometric pattern? This is a Mode 6 inheritance question
>    posed in materials-science vocabulary.
>
> 4. **Mineral-templated self-assembly (Cairns-Smith clay hypothesis,
>    Wächtershäuser pyrite-pulled metabolism):** Do these proposals
>    invoke any mechanism by which a mineral template's information
>    propagates to descendant minerals? Most relevant for origin-of-
>    life implications.
>
> 5. **Spectrin-actin cortex and other membrane-associated 2D
>    patterns:** Do these patterns inherit at cell division, and if so
>    is the inheritance lineage-level (M1-class) or individual-level
>    (M3/M4-class)?
>
> 6. **Lipid-bilayer composition templating:** Do daughter cells
>    inherit the parent's lipid composition pattern beyond what is
>    encoded in the genome's lipid biosynthesis enzymes?
>
> For each candidate system, extract:
> - (a) the templating substrate's physical nature
> - (b) whether the substrate's *templating capacity* (not just its
>   geometric pattern) is inherited at lineage division
> - (c) the H3-class assignment: M0 (no inheritance), M1 (lineage
>   fixation, no variation), M2 (lineage fixation with re-draw), or
>   higher class
> - (d) experimental evidence for the assignment, including any
>   reconstitution work
>
> Time window 1985–present. Include speculative origin-of-life
> proposals (Cairns-Smith, Wächtershäuser) but mark them as
> theoretical rather than reconstituted. Exclude pure structural-
> biology papers that don't address the inheritance question.
>
> The most informative outcomes:
> - If most systems classify as M0: the framework's "Mode 6 is non-
>   inheriting" claim is empirically supported, v3's Mode 6 paragraph
>   is unambiguous.
> - If most systems classify as M1: the framework needs to weaken
>   its claim to "Mode 6 cannot develop M4-class inheritance," and
>   v3 should reframe Mode 6 as "weak inheritance" rather than "no
>   inheritance."
> - If any system shows evidence of M2 or M3-class behavior: that's
>   a substantive surprise that v3 should engage with directly.
>
> The search is not asking whether biological surface templating
> exists in principle — it asks whether the templating *capacity*
> (the ability to template new copies) is heritable in the H3 sense.

## How to use this prompt

Paste the prompt into Sciscape. Output goes to
`/storage/kiran-stuff/templating_framework/lit_search/D6_sharpened_<date>.md`.

When results return, classify each hit by H3 mechanism class and
update v3 Discussion's Mode 6 paragraph based on the empirical
distribution. The dispatch H6 (real Mode 6 simulation) cannot be
written until this returns — H6 needs to know which mechanism class
to simulate.

If no results return that distinguish M0 from M1 for any biological
Mode 6 candidate, that's also a finding: the inheritance question
hasn't been asked of these systems experimentally, and v3 should
flag it as an open question.
